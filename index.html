Resilient Against the Tide: One-Flippered Olive Ridley Nests at Rushikulya.

Rushikulya Rookery, Odisha | 24 Feb 2025

In the early morning of 24th February, an Olive Ridley turtle with a missing left front flipper was spotted nesting at Rushikulya Rookery. The cause of her lost flipper is unknown—possibly an injury from predators or fishing nets. Yet, she successfully laid her eggs, proving the resilience of marine life.

This sighting highlights the challenges sea turtles face and the importance of conservation efforts. The turtle’s successful nesting, Despite her missing flipper, symbolizes resilience. It serves as a reminder that even with the threats they face, sea turtles can survive if we continue protecting their habitats. Thanks to the dedicated work of ZSI, Wildlife Trust Of India, WWF India, Dakshin Foundation, Tata Steel Foundation, Olive Ridley Project, M S Swaminathan Research Foundation, ATREE, The Odisha Forest Department and other organizations for their tireless work in protecting these endangered species and preserving vital nesting habitats. Creating awareness about sea turtle conservation has grown tremendously. Their continuous efforts in educating local communities, conducting research, and implementing protection measures have led to a record-breaking mass nesting (arribada) this year at Rushikulya rookery.

A heartfelt thank you to the local fishing communities and residents, whose increased understanding and support have played a crucial role in protecting these turtles.
